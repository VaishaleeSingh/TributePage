Sushant Singh Rajput

1986-2020

Forever in our hearts

Sushant Singh Rajput was more than just a Bollywood star; he was a symbol of passion and versatility. From his breakout role as MS Dhoni to his explorations in science and advocacy for mental health, he left an indelible mark on cinema and society. His infectious smile and genuine curiosity touched hearts worldwide, making him not just an actor, but an inspiration. Though gone too soon, his legacy as a talented performer and compassionate human being lives on, forever cherished by those who admired him.

Biography

  • Early Life: Born on January 21, 1986, in Patna, Bihar, India. Raised in Delhi, he pursued Mechanical Engineering at the Delhi Technological University (formerly Delhi College of Engineering).
  • Entry into Acting: Started his career as a background dancer and later became a television actor. Made his acting debut in the TV show "Kis Desh Mein Hai Meraa Dil" (2008).
  • Breakthrough TV Role: Gained popularity with the role of Manav in the TV serial "Pavitra Rishta" (2009-2011).
  • Transition to Films: Debuted in Bollywood with "Kai Po Che!" (2013), based on Chetan Bhagat's novel "The 3 Mistakes of My Life". Followed by notable films like "Shuddh Desi Romance" (2013), "PK" (2014), and "Detective Byomkesh Bakshy!" (2015).
  • Critical Acclaim and Success: Received acclaim for his portrayal of Indian cricketer Mahendra Singh Dhoni in the biopic "M.S. Dhoni: The Untold Story" (2016).
  • Other Ventures: Founded a company named Innsaei Ventures focused on emerging technology and content production.
  • Personal Life and Tragic Death: Sushant Singh Rajput passed away on June 14, 2020, at his residence in Mumbai. His death was ruled as suicide by hanging, sparking debates and investigations related to mental health issues and pressures in the entertainment industry.
  • Legacy: Remembered for his versatile acting skills, intelligence, and passion for science and technology. Fans and colleagues continue to celebrate his contributions to Indian cinema and advocate for mental health awareness.
